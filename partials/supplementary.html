RoDTEP · Missed Declaration

Forgot to declare RoDTEP on the shipping bill? Options to recover

If your shipping bill was filed with RoDTEP marked "N" (No) or the declaration was missed, the claim doesn't auto-process. It's harder to recover — but not always lost. Here are the possible routes.

What went wrong

The RoDTEP benefit is granted only when a declaration of intent is made on the shipping bill at the time of export. If it was set to "N" or left out, the system does not credit the incentive.

Possible recovery routes

  1. Shipping bill amendment (Section 149) — Request Customs to amend the shipping bill under Section 149 of the Customs Act, supported by documents proving eligibility.
  2. Check for supplementary claim windows — DGFT/CBIC occasionally allow rectification/supplementary claims for specific periods; check the latest notifications.
  3. Represent to the jurisdictional Customs — File a written representation via the Officer Interface with supporting evidence.
  4. Fix it going forward — Always mark the RoDTEP declaration correctly on every future shipping bill.
Honest note: a missed declaration is the hardest case to recover and success isn't guaranteed. Act quickly and keep every supporting document.
