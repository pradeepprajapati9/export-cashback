IGST refund denied for higher duty drawback — what to do

Your IGST refund was blocked only because you claimed the higher rate of duty drawback (the "A" suffix)? Courts have repeatedly held this denial is not valid. Here's the position and your route.

What happened

Under CBIC Circular 37/2018, the automated system blocks the IGST refund if a higher duty drawback rate was claimed. But multiple High Courts — including the Madras High Court (2026) and earlier the Gujarat High Court — have ruled that an administrative circular and system validation cannot override your statutory right to the IGST refund.

Your route to recover

  1. Confirm the reason — Verify the refund was denied only due to the higher drawback claim, not another defect.
  2. Prepare a representation — Cite the Madras HC 2026 and Gujarat HC rulings that IGST refund can't be denied on this ground.
  3. File with jurisdictional Customs — Submit the representation via the Officer Interface / proper officer for reconsideration.
  4. Escalate if needed — If rejected, a writ petition following these precedents is a proven path.
Good news: this is one of the strongest cases — the courts are consistently on the exporter's side.
